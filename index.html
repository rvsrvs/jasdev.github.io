About

I’m an engineer, writer, and reader in New York who is interested in human performance, cognitive science, and cycling.

Currently, I spend my days building Peloton. Previously, I built APIs and iOS apps at Tumblr, Imgur, and the Recurse Center (F1 ’15). Before that, I interned at Twitter (KPCB ’13), Fitocracy, and Microsoft while studying mathematics at UVA (SEAS ’14).

If you want to go below the surface, click here.

Thoughts (RSS)

An attempt to create a public, high-cadence log of what’s on my mind. Along the spectrum between my Twitter account and my blog, this journal sits in-between. Unpolished thoughts, developed over time.

Essays (RSS, email)

Writing is a way for me to distill thoughts. The intent of these essays isn’t to teach (I’ve learned that advice is extremely lossy), but rather to reflect and think aloud. While most of these pieces have been drafted alone, it takes a village to publish.
